In conclusion, there can be no difinitive answers from this data set. Our data is incomplete, we only have 891 passenger records of the 2224 passengers on board. We also do not know details of the relationships between people, if the relationship was between siblings or spouses or who exactly was the child or parent. Despit all of this, I can find some trends in the data. I am surprised at the extent of how unequally men were treated compared to their female co-passengers. The low rates of survival at all classes for fathers on the sinking ship is shocking, which makes me wonder about the demographics of the men who did survive. I found it pretty interesting how 3 lateral relationships(sibling-spouse) on board played a critical role in survival for the third class. From this data, we can pose hypotheses for further research that strong sibling-spouse relationships and gender played important roles in surviving the sinking of the Titanic. To make definitive conclusions, eye witness recounts of events in third class, as well as a more complete dataset on all the passengers and their relatioships to one another would be needed.
